Fetal Wishing Service Station

2020

Immersive Interactive Installation

Fetal Wishing Service Station is an immersive interactive installation. Inspired by the Fetal Wishing Guanyin in Chinese Buddhism, by creating a space to realize the desire to have children, this artwork ironically exposes the common oppression of reproduction toward women in society. A mobile service station for fetal wishing was created using two dragon pillars, a LED advertisement lightbox, two TV screens, a computer, a surveillance camera, and religious decorations. When people use four religious gestures in the station, the machine learning program “Teachable Machine” on the computer recognizes the gestures through the camera and plays videos on the TV. There are four videos that correspond to four kinds of fertility oppressions toward females: pregnancy, abortion, brainwashing, and gender regulations. In addition, the missing opponent in the station – the statue, reveals that the utilitarian nature of praying and worshipping is nothing but the projection of human’s own wishes.
